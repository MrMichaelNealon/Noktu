Qubes - A block-based puzzle game

Gameplay

I tried to describe the gameplay to my mate and he was confused, not that it's terribly difficult to confuse him, but still I had to agree - despite my best efforts I had failed to deliver a reasonable description.

Now you would think maybe it's too complex or simply makes no sense, but the truth is that it all became clearer to him when he finally played it - the gameplay is actually familiar and quite intuitive.

It's a Tetris clone!

It's not! It has similar gameplay but there are some very important differences.

We begin on level 1 with 5 lives - to begin with we are presented with a simple design that depicts a cube with 7 layers of depth, each individual layer is a 7x7 grid of tiles. the front and back layers are the most important - the layers in between being nothing more than sort of transportation layers.

Blocks which are made up of between 1 and 4 tiles will form on the back layer. Qubes has a level of 3-dimensional depth, we can think of the layer that is furthest away as the back layer on which new blocks will form. Naturally, the blocks and tiles look smaller on the back layer than they do at the front and the front layer has some opacity so that we can easily see new tiles form on the back layer.

When the blocks initially appear they are red in colour, they will then change to amber then green. During this initial stage, before the block turns green and locks we can move it around the back layer. Once it turns green and locks we can no longer move the block and it will start to fall towards the front layer. The front layer is the layer that is closest to us - the layer that the falling blocks will land on. The object is to complete rows and columns of tiles on the front layer, the completed lines will then clear and increase our score. We cannot land a tile on top of another tile, this results in a clash in which case both tiles disappear and we lose a life.

Yeah, a Tetris clone!

No! Firstly we cannot rotate a block while it is unlocked, we can only move it - we can shift it up or down, but not rotate it. What we can do is rotate the front layer while the locked block is falling. So a additional element to the gameplay is trying to figure out where to position a block then rotate the screen orientation to make it fit and complete more lines... and it is very challenging!

As we continue to play and clear more lines we will level up and the game will get faster, levelling up will award you an additional life but you can start to lose them in rapid succession.
